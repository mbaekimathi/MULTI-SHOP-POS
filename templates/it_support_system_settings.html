Point of sale settings

Cart modes, customer fields, tax on totals, discounts, compulsory printing, and which printer connection types POS may use — receipt layout stays under Receipt format.

Cart type (POS workflow)

Pick exactly one mode (toggle one on — the other turns off automatically). Direct POS finalizes each cart at checkout (current behavior — no save / reopen). Withhold POS lets staff save (hold) the current cart as an open order — items are deducted from shop stock or kitchen portions when held (per POS inventory below), and a company receipt is printed for the saved batch only if compulsory printing is on. Staff can then start other orders, return to update a held order (each update prints a company receipt for the new lines and deducts only those), and complete the sale as usual when the customer pays. On the shop POS cart, staff choose Direct sale (normal checkout) or Hold tab (save & hold) for each cart; when company mode is Direct POS below, only direct checkout is available.

Direct POS

Single cart per checkout — finalize, print, and clear.

Withhold POS

Save (hold) the cart; deduct stock per save; reopen to add more items; finalize payment when the customer is ready.

Transactional types (POS cart)

At least one must stay on or checkout cannot complete — if all three would be off, Sale turns back on automatically. When only one is on, staff do not see a transaction-type row in the cart; POS uses that mode automatically.

Sale

Cash / M-Pesa checkout (records a completed sale).

Credit

Credit checkout and related POS flows.

Quotation

Quote-only checkout (lead / no inventory movement).

Payment methods (cash sale)

At least one must stay on — if all three would be off, Cash turns back on automatically. When only one method is enabled, checkout uses it without showing a payment row in the cart.

Cash

Full amount paid in cash.

M-Pesa

Full amount on M-Pesa.

Cash + M-Pesa (split)

Split tender between cash and M-Pesa.

Buy items (POS header)

When on, staff see Buy items in the POS header to record stock purchased from sellers. Stock-in stays available from stock management whether this is off or on.

Customer details (cash sale)

When off, the customer phone/name block is hidden for normal sales. Credit and quotation still show customer fields when needed.

Cart: amount sets quantity

When on, each cart line shows a money Amount field instead of quantity. POS divides by the unit price to set quantity (respects stock caps). Lines with zero unit price keep the quantity field. − / + still adjust by one unit of quantity.

Tax in POS totals

When on (or Include tax on receipt under Receipt format), the tax rate set there is added to sale, credit, and quotation amounts — you must enter a tax rate on that tab. When both this and that option are off, cart and checkout use line totals with no tax.

POS inventory

Default for all branches: pick exactly one mode (toggle one on — the others turn off automatically). Shop stock deducts shelf quantity at checkout; Kitchen portions deducts portion counts; Both deducts portions at checkout while shelf quantities are maintained separately for items registered under Stock management. A shop can override this under Shop settings → POS printing when “Customize POS printing for this shop” is on.

Shop stock

Deduct shelf quantity on sale.

Kitchen portions

Deduct portion counts on sale.

Both (kitchen + shelf)

Checkout uses portions; shelf qty via Stock management for registered items.

Discounts (edit line prices)

When on, cashiers can change the unit price of each line in the cart.

Printer section

Choose whether staff may pair Bluetooth, network, or USB printers on the shop POS. You can allow printers but leave Printing compulsory on sale off so checkout does not require a saved printer. If all three types are off, compulsory printing turns off automatically (nothing to configure).

Printing compulsory on sale

When on, staff cannot complete standard sale checkout (6-digit code) until POS treats the printer as ready: network or USB need a profile saved in the shop’s Printer panel; Bluetooth needs that profile saved and Connect printer so the header shows linked (saved alone is not enough). Credit and quotation flows are not blocked. The server also rejects recording a standard sale if this shop has no saved printer profile.
